Prospecting Playbook
B2B best practices baked into every signal, briefing, and outreach
The Core Framework
Trigger-Based Only — Only reach out when something happened. No trigger = no signal worth pursuing. The trigger is your reason to exist in their inbox.
The 90-Day Window — New executives have 90 days to prove themselves. They buy external advisory fastest in the first 60 days. An exec hire is your single best signal. Act within 1 week.
Multi-Thread Always — Never rely on one contact. Identify the economic buyer (approves budget), the champion (uses the service), and the potential blocker. Reach all three independently.
Pain + Budget + Authority — A lead only converts when all three exist. No budget = waste of time. No authority = long cycle. No pain = no urgency.
Outreach Rules That Work
Pattern Interrupt — First line must break the pattern. Never: "I hope this finds you well." Never: "I came across your profile." Start with an insight or a specific observation about their situation.
Give Before You Ask — Every first touch includes a gift: a relevant insight, a benchmark, a framework, a stat they haven't seen. You get a response by being useful.
One Ask Per Touch — Never ask for a meeting AND a response AND a call. Pick one. "Worth a conversation?" beats "Can we schedule 30 minutes next week?"
LinkedIn DM Under 150 Words — People read DMs on mobile. Every sentence must earn its place.
Follow-Ups Add Value — Never say "following up" or "circling back." Every follow-up touch adds a new piece of value — a case study, an insight, a relevant stat.
Entry Point Strategy
Executive Workshop ($18-30k) — Best cold entry. 1-2 days, fixed scope, clear deliverable. Easy to approve. Creates dependency for the next engagement.
Discovery Sprint ($15-20k) — Best for companies mid-transition. 30-45 days, audit + roadmap + investment case. Almost always sells itself into a retainer.
Retainer (any tier) — Best sold after a Sprint or Workshop proves value. Never cold. Always earned.
Signals That Predict Fast Closes
New exec + hiring surge together — New leader building their team. Needs strategic direction before the team runs in 10 directions. 30-day close window.
Bad quarter + board pressure — Budget unlocked by pain. Decision maker needs to show action fast. Fastest close cycle in B2B advisory.
PE-backed + acquisition — PE partner is the real buyer. Entry via the operating partner relationship if possible. One relationship = multiple portfolio companies.
Funding + DTC/digital pivot — Budget to spend, direction unclear, no internal expertise. Easiest Discovery Sprint pitch.
What to Avoid
Companies mid-layoff — No budget, survival mode. Wait 6 months.
Major consulting firm already engaged — If Deloitte, McKinsey, or Accenture is on record, the decision is made. Don't compete — wait for the contract to end.
No identifiable decision maker — If you can't find who owns digital/commercial strategy, the org is too opaque to sell into cold.
