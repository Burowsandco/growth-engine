Prospecting Playbook
Best practices from Clay, Apollo, and top B2B sales frameworks
Signal Sources — Where to Find Real Triggers
LinkedIn — Job postings (hiring surge), executive announcements (new role), company posts (product launches, expansions). Most reliable for exec hire signals.
Crunchbase / PitchBook — Funding rounds, acquisitions, PE ownership, investor details. Best for funding and M&A signals.
Google News / PR Newswire — Earnings misses, press releases, expansion announcements. Best for bad quarter and new product signals.
Glassdoor / Indeed / LinkedIn Jobs — Volume and types of roles posted. 5+ digital/marketing roles in 30 days = hiring surge signal.
D&B Hoovers / ZoomInfo — Company firmographics, revenue, employee count, tech stack, decision maker contact info.
SEC EDGAR — Public company filings, 10-K/10-Q for revenue misses, restructuring, major strategic changes.
G2 / Capterra — Tech stack signals. A company switching CRM or marketing platforms signals a transformation moment.
Email Finding — Best Practice Approach
The engine generates 3 format guesses based on common corporate patterns. Use Apollo, Hunter.io, or Clearbit to verify before sending. Verification is free for small volumes.
Format 1: firstname@company.com (most common, ~40% of companies)
Format 2: first.last@company.com (~30% of companies)
Format 3: flast@company.com (~20% of companies)
Click any email guess to copy it. Verify at hunter.io/email-verifier before sending to avoid bounces.
Clay / Apollo Best Practices Applied Here
Trigger-first waterfall (Clay method) — Identify the trigger first, then find the person, then write the message. Never start with a list of names and work backwards.
Multi-source enrichment (Apollo method) — Every signal is cross-referenced: company website + LinkedIn + news + funding data. A signal only surfaces if it can be validated from at least one public source.
Intent + timing = urgency score — ICP score reflects fit. Urgency (Hot/Warm/New) reflects timing. Hot = act this week. The combination drives your prioritization.
Personalization at scale — Every outreach message references the specific trigger. Never a generic template. The source URL tells you exactly what to reference in your message.
The Core Framework
Trigger-Based Only — No trigger = no outreach. The trigger is your reason to exist in their inbox.
The 90-Day Window — New executives have 90 days to prove themselves. Act within 1 week of the announcement.
Pattern Interrupt — Never start with "I hope this finds you well." Reference the specific trigger in your first sentence.
Entry Point First — Discovery Sprint or Executive Workshop before any retainer conversation. Prove value first.
